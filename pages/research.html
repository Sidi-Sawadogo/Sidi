Effects of Subsidies on Welfare and Market Structure in the US Broadband Industry

This paper addresses the issue of efficient estimation of a parameter of interest defined by a conditional moment restriction. Such a framework involves many potential unconditional moment conditions based on splines or other approximating functions. The benchmark estimator we consider is the continuous updating GMM estimator (CUE) due to its relatively low bias with many moment conditions. e introduce a Ridge-type regularized version of CUE (RCUE) to deal with the singularity problem of the weighting matrix that can arise when using many moments. We show that the RCUE is consistent and asymptotically normal and reaches the semi-parametric efficiency bound when the regularization parameter tends to zero at a certain rate. R egularization allows us to get rid of any restriction on the growth rate of the number of moment c onditions required in previous literature, so that one can use more moments than the sample size to gain efficiency in practice. We illustrate in a Monte Carlo exercise the relevance of our estimator in small sample.
